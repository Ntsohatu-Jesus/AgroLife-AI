MAIZE FARMING IN DRY SEASON (CAMEROON/AFRICA CONTEXT):

- Choose early maturing maize seeds (75–90 days)
- Plant at the beginning of the dry season when there is still some soil moisture
- Use mulch (dry grass, leaves) around crops to reduce water loss
- Water crops early in the morning (6–8am) or late evening (5–7pm)
- Avoid watering under hot sun to prevent evaporation
- Use organic manure (cow dung, compost) to improve soil moisture retention
- If possible, use simple irrigation methods like buckets, watering cans, or drip bottles
- Plant maize in rows with proper spacing (75cm between rows, 25–30cm between plants)
- Weed regularly to reduce competition for water
- Protect crops from pests like armyworms using ash or local organic pesticides

TOMATO FARMING (COMMON PRACTICES):

- Use raised beds to prevent waterlogging
- Space plants properly to avoid disease spread
- Water consistently but do not overwater
- Yellow leaves may indicate lack of nitrogen
- Use compost or poultry manure to enrich soil
- Stake plants to keep fruits off the ground

GENERAL FARMING PRACTICES:

- Always observe soil condition before planting
- Rotate crops (e.g., maize → beans) to maintain soil nutrients
- Use local organic fertilizers when chemical fertilizers are expensive
- Practice mixed cropping to reduce risk of total loss
- Harvest rainwater where possible for dry season use

PEST CONTROL (LOCAL METHODS):

- Use wood ash around plants to repel insects
- Use neem leaves or neem water as natural pesticide
- Remove infected plants early to prevent spread

FARMER SAFETY (BASIC):

- In case of snake bite:
  - Stay calm and limit movement
  - Do NOT cut the wound
  - Do NOT suck the venom
  - Try to reach nearest health center immediately

- For cuts:
  - Wash with clean water
  - Apply clean cloth or antiseptic if available
